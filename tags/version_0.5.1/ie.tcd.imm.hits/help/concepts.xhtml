Basic concepts

This section introduce some of the basic concepts used in HiTS.

Well

The fundamental experimental unit, which can contain either raw or processed data. In this model it may contain

  • samples,
  • positive controls,
  • negatitive controls,
  • or any other type

of experimental results.

The layout of wells should follow a (8 rows × 12 columns) [96] or a (16 rows × 24 columns) [384] grid, called physical plate.

Plate

The plate is an abstract concept in cellHTS2. It represents a group of physical plates with the same well layout.

Replicate

Also an abstract concept. It represents a group of physical plates which may have different layouts, but each group should have the same layouts in the same order.

Parameter

For each well you may have different parameters describing their content (on each physical plate). These parameters are handled independently through the whole process.

Mathematical concepts

This section shows some basic concepts and notations used in other parts of the program.

Mathematical notations

The pos, neg, sample in superscript means selection of the positive or negative controls, or the samples from a group of wells.

The p means a plate group, r means a replicate group, w means selection by well position.

When you see one of the aggregating functions having in subscript one or two of these notations it will mean that those are fixed for a value and only the others are aggregated (selected by that/those groups).

General overview

The log transform is performed on individual values.

The normalisation is done by physical plates.

The variance adjustment can be done by physical plates or by replicate groups, or it may be omitted.

Scoring is done by replicate groups by default, but you can choose per physical plates too for Z scores.

The summarisation of replicate values are computed by each plate groups of individual wells.

When log transform selected the additive normalisation will be used.

log

Simple 2 based logarithm.

additive normalisation

In case of average like normalisation methods (mean, median, shorth, negatives) the aggregated value is subtracted from each value. Its natural point (no change value to the reference) is 0.

multiplicative normalisation

In case of average like normalisation methods (mean, median, shorth, negatives) each value is divided by the aggregated value. Its natural point (no change value to the reference) is 1.

Other mathematical concepts

Quarter 1

When computing the four quarters this value is between the lowest and the second lowest quarters.

Quarter 3

When computing the four quarters this value is between the second highest and the highest quarters.
